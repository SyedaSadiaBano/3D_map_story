The Rising Tide: A 3D View of Sea-Level Rise on the Sindh Coast

A projection of coastal flood risk for 2030, 2040, and 2050 based on IPCC AR6 data.

Introduction: A Coastline at Risk

The coastline of Sindh faces a significant threat from global sea-level rise. This 3D story map visualizes the progressive expansion of areas vulnerable to coastal flooding over the next three decades.

2030: The Baseline Risk

By 2030, areas at or near the current mean sea level are considered at high risk. The light blue area shows the extent of this baseline vulnerability.

2040: Expanding Vulnerability

As the sea level rises, the zone of vulnerability expands. The medium blue area shows the 50-meter buffer zone now susceptible to storm surges.

2050: A Critical Threshold

By mid-century, the flood risk zone expands to 150 meters from the baseline (dark blue area), highlighting the urgent need for long-term adaptation.
